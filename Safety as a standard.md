Turner gave the industry an AI safety tool for free. I see this as a category strategy.

SafeT Coach works inside ChatGPT and pulls from Turner's EHS policies, OSHA standards, and 25,000 test interactions worth of institutional knowledge. Workers can snap a photo of a site condition and get advice on what they're looking at. It came from Turner's internal programme — when they rolled out enterprise ChatGPT without central management, workers built over 400 custom apps. SafeT Coach was one of them.

In *Firms Must Stop Buying Software and Start Building Platforms*, I wrote about Turner as an example of exactly this: invest in a broad technology layer and let specific solutions emerge from it. SafeT Coach is the product of that approach.

Trace Cohen argued recently that Palantir won by owning the translation layer between how organisations actually operate and how software requires them to operate.

**A widely adopted, free safety AI would occupy the same position in construction — not just a tool, but the standard for what machines understand "safe" to mean.**

That position compounds. Increased trust drives adoption. Adoption drives data. Data makes the system better. And a better system becomes harder to replicate.

From building tools for themselves to setting the standard for everyone else. Whether intentional or not, this starts to look like a different kind of move.
