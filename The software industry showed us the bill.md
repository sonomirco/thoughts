Microsoft cancelled its internal Claude Code licences because token-based billing made the cost untenable. Uber burned through its entire 2026 AI budget in four months. Across the tech sector, flat-rate plans are being replaced with usage-based billing and prices are rising by up to 37%.

**This is not a Silicon Valley problem. It is a preview.**

Construction's internal AI strategy needs to price this in before it arrives, otherwise what you gain in time saved you spend on the bill for the model you used. This is also how the AI startups are starting to address it in the architecture around the model: pre-sort 100% of client data down to the 1% that actually matters, then let the expensive model touch only that. Don't bring a knife to a gunfight.

But the cost problem and the quality problem share the same root, and most of the industry is only looking at one of them.

In most cases that is not a technology observation, it is a usage one. Uploading a fifty-page document to answer a simple question, running vague prompts without a defined objective, treating AI like a search engine with better grammar — these habits burn tokens and produce mediocre output, which confirms the scepticism and compounds the cost at the same time.

The people getting results share one habit: they know exactly what problem they want to solve before they open a prompt. They audit the output. They pre-filter the input. They understand enough about how the model thinks to ask it a properly scoped question.

The software industry scaled usage and is now reviewing budgets. Construction has a chance to build the usage capability and the cost discipline together, before the subsidy phase ends.

The gap the industry calls a technology limitation is, in most cases, a usage limitation. Those are different problems, and the second one is more within reach than most firms have tried to find out.

Sean Young at NVIDIA has been living this question from the inside. His team exceeded their token allocation in the first year of Jensen's "spend tokens worth half your salary" experiment. His read on what that actually revealed — about the difference between token burn and useful output, and what it means for how AEC prices its services — is the AEC-specific version of everything above.
